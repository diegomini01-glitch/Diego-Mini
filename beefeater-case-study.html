Integrated Campaign

Beefeater Limón

The challenge

A legacy spirits brand looking to reposition for Gen Z and younger millennials in a category dominated by craft cocktails and ready-to-drink alternatives. The brief: an integrated campaign across earned, owned, and paid media that breaks beyond traditional spirits advertising and earns cultural relevance.

The insight

Gen Z doesn't drink the way previous generations did. They drink less, they drink more deliberately, and they share the experience before the taste. The opportunity wasn't to sell more gin; it was to create a gin moment worth posting about. The Limón variant gave us a sensory anchor: a flavor cue that could carry a brand story without leaning on heritage.

The concept: Lemon Drop Pop Up

A multi-touchpoint activation centered on the 'Lemon Drop Pop Up': a recurring, location-specific experiential event combining physical sampling, AI-powered confessional stands, and a gamified TikTok filter that turns participation into shareable content. The campaign operates across three integrated channels: earned (PR + influencer organic reach), owned (TikTok filter + brand-owned event nights), and paid (geo-targeted social and venue media).

Channel strategy

The integrated channel mix:

  • Earned: PR seeding, influencer organic engagement at pop-up events, user-generated content amplification
  • Owned: Gamified TikTok filter, brand-owned event nights, AI confessional stands generating shareable content
  • Paid: Geo-targeted social activation around pop-up cities, programmatic display, venue media partnerships
  • Experiential: "Lemon Drop Pop Up" as the physical anchor, sensory engagement first, brand association second

Budget allocation

Across digital and physical touchpoints, the campaign deployed a €500k budget with deliberate allocation: experiential activation as the lead investment, social-first content production as the volume driver, and paid amplification calibrated to extend organic reach in priority cities. The detailed budget breakdown is in the deck, available on request.

What this work demonstrates

  • Integrated marketing communication across earned, owned, and paid media
  • Experiential marketing strategy for a Gen Z audience
  • Cultural-relevance positioning for legacy brands
  • Multi-channel campaign orchestration with measurable engagement mechanics
  • Strategic budget allocation across digital and physical touchpoints
