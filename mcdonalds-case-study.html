Strategic ESG Proposal

McDonald's Spain

Role Team contributor · Researcher
Context IE Business School · MSMC
Brief Strategic ESG communications proposal
Team 7 students · 2025

McDonald's wordmarks and brand visuals are not displayed on this public page. This was an academic team project; no engagement with McDonald's took place. The full deck is available on request under NDA.

The challenge

McDonald's Spain holds the leading position in the quick-service restaurant market, with 630 locations, 70%+ locally sourced ingredients, and a recognized employer brand. Yet among Gen Z and Millennial consumers, the brand faced a growing perception gap: real environmental action, poorly communicated, losing the credibility battle against competitors that were louder, not better. The brief was to close that gap with a data-driven communications and brand strategy.

Research foundation

The strategy was built on four layers of primary research conducted across Spain:

  • Quantitative survey: 239 responses from Gen Z and Millennials (ages 18–45), measuring trust, awareness, and purchase behavior around sustainability claims
  • Semi-structured interviews: 15 in-depth sessions (ages 20–40), exploring emotional responses to McDonald's sustainability messaging versus competitors
  • Focus group: Generation Alpha (12-year-olds, Madrid), testing sustainability concepts and brand messaging comprehension
  • Observational research: In-store fieldwork across McDonald's Spain locations, assessing visibility and clarity of environmental communications

Key finding

78.5% of respondents expected quick-service brands to act on sustainability. McDonald's ranked below key competitors (Burger King, KFC, Telepizza, Subway) in perceived environmental credibility, despite having the strongest actual programs. The perception gap was not about what McDonald's was doing; it was about what consumers could see and feel in the moment. The highest-traffic customer touchpoint (the ordering experience) was the most under-utilized channel for ESG communication.

The concept: gamified sustainability in the customer journey

A gamified sustainability platform embedded in the ordering experience: connecting product choices, tray liner interactions, and app-based repeat visits to a visible, rewarding sustainability track tied to McDonald's loyalty program. The design principle: make sustainability participation the path of least resistance, not a separate campaign. Each touchpoint in the customer journey from ordering screen to packaging to app becomes a moment of environmental storytelling that accumulates into measurable brand affinity.

Communications strategy

The proposal delivered a full communications architecture across four areas:

  • Audience mapping: Segmented messaging across Gen Z, Millennials, and Spanish families, each with distinct trust triggers, channel preferences, and sustainability expectations
  • Messaging architecture: Shifted from claims-based ("we are sustainable") to evidence-based ("here's what happened at this restaurant this week") storytelling
  • Channel plan: Prioritized in-store (ordering screens, packaging, tray liners), app (loyalty mechanics, progress tracking), and earned media (local supplier stories, community partnerships)
  • KPI framework: Linked ESG participation rates, in-app engagement, and brand perception scores to measure the strategy's impact on trust and commercial performance

Competitive context

The proposal included a perception analysis benchmarking McDonald's against Burger King, KFC, Telepizza, and Subway across sustainability credibility, communication frequency, and consumer recall of specific initiatives. McDonald's led on actual programs but lagged on visibility, particularly among 18–34 consumers who form the core of the brand's growth segment in Spain.

What this work demonstrates

  • Primary research design across quantitative, qualitative, and observational methods
  • ESG positioning strategy for a high-visibility consumer brand under reputational pressure
  • Audience segmentation and stakeholder messaging across Gen Z, Millennials, and families
  • Brand perception gap analysis connecting research findings to actionable communications architecture
  • Competitive benchmarking and KPI design linking brand trust to commercial performance
